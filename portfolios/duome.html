Duome's Silidisc is a medical device designed to prevent the formation of abdominal adhesions during surgery. Adhesions are scar tissues that form after even the most benign adbominal surgeries (apendectomies, C-sections, etc.) and lead to a lifetime of risk of small bowel obstruction (SBO). SBO causes 67,000 hospitalizations, 2,500 deaths, and $2.15 billion in costs in the United States each year. The process of removing this tissue, called adhesiolysis, often results in more adhesions and a vicious cycle of dangerous surgeries.

The Silidisc looks to prevent adhesions by improving the surgical abcess environment to control moisture and temperature. In addition to manufacturing the device itself (CAD, 3D-printed molds, silicone casting, post-processing), we combined manufacturing and electrical components (CAD, laser cutting, circuit-board design, Arduino data collection) to build a test rig and eventually proved efficacy in a porcine model experiment.
